Case Study · 2024 · Solo

FEEDO

A deep dive into designing trust as architecture.

01 / Context

The loss of a tangible connection

Many people want to help, but abandon digital donation processes in frustration. Through my own experience and 20 qualitative user interviews, three core pain points emerged: deep distrust of organizations, high cognitive friction in the donation flow, and complete information silence after the transaction.

"
„My 5 Euros won't change anything anyway."

This quote from a study participant reveals: the problem isn't lack of empathy — it's the broken link between humanitarian action and visible result.

02 / Strategy

How do you design systemic trust?

Primary persona Anna (32, mobile-first, skeptical) wants to help — but doesn't trust classic structures. The entire project is built around one core question:

How Might We:

show Anna that an animal welfare organization can be transparent, approachable, and truly effective?

Conceptual Shift

The solution lives in semantics. FEEDO translates abstract Euro amounts into logistical reality. „15 Euro donation" becomes the tangible promise: „5 days of care secured". The word „Vorrat" (supply) builds the perfect bridge between emotional empathy for the animal and transparent storage logistics in the shelter's reality.

03 / Interface Architecture

Four pillars of the UI structure

The interface separates functional lightness from emotional depth. Each pillar is a clear architectural decision:

1.

Lazy Registration

Frictionless 45-second checkout via Apple Pay — no account creation required. Positive experience first, optional registration after.

2.

Feeding Slider & Snack Button

Interactive lever (minimum step 3 € = 1 day of nourishment) and a playful, pressure-free snack-bone (+1 €) for quick micro-interactions.

3.

Versorgung (Care)

Dedicated tab inside the animal card for long-term collective needs (medications, blankets) with clear community progress bars.

4.

Priorität (Emergency Tab)

Global, sober space for acute medical crises. To preserve seriousness, gamification is deliberately absent — fixed amounts, clear donation trackers.

04 / Validation & Iteration

Managing the emotional peak

Qualitative validation with 5 test participants revealed: a full-screen chat right after the payment impulse caused irritation and blocked the natural flow.

Iteration

Before / After: the full-screen chat was replaced with an elegant half-screen widget (bottom sheet). It holds the emotional wave but can be effortlessly dismissed. Critical fix: missing currency symbols on the snack button created skepticism. Now the € sign is animated directly on the button.

„Trust isn't a step in the flow.
Trust is the flow."
